What is Songket?

Songket is a traditional Malaysian handwoven fabric made from silk or cotton, patterned with gold or silver threads. Derived from the Malay word “sungkit” meaning “to hook”, the name reflects its weaving method of hooking and slipping metallic threads into the fabric.

STAR WITH LARGE CHEST MOTIF

This motif is interpreted as a symbol of well-being and peace in living together. This symbol is also associated with hope and balance, and reflects good character, polite words, and peaceful life in a community.

BOUQUET MOTIF

The motif symbolizes love, purity, and refinement of character, as well as friendship and brotherhood. This motif is from flora such as flowers, often used to represent beauty, happiness, and fertility.

SIDE-BY-SIDE BOUQUET MOTIF

This motif refers to the hope of having an equal and corresponding position financially and spiritually, as well as symbolizing peace and success. This motif is usually found in Malay carving and weaving as a philosophy that teaches equality and harmony.
